{a's Clam Chowder

Home Food Dranks
  • 1 large can peeled tomatoes ( I buy about 64 oz of canned crushed tomatoes, it is usually 2 cans)
  • 1 med can tomato juice (about 32 oz )
  • 5 strips of bacon, diced
  • 2 large onions (I buy chopped frozen onions usually a coupe of packages estimating the amount of large onions)
  • 4 large stalks of celery diced
  • 2 large carrots ( I buy peeled baby carrots and chop them into small rounds)
  • 4 large potatoes peeled and diced
  • 2 dozen raw whole clams (I buy 3 lbs of raw frozen chopped clams because it is VERY hard to chop raw clams and canned chop clams are no good)
  • 3/4 tsp oregano
  • 2 to 4 bottles of clam juice
  • 1 small green pepper diced

This recipe comes from a friend of my mother's. Mimi and I modified it over the years as some of the ingredients are now available already chopped up, etc. We also added more clams to enrich the soup and never use water where the recipe calls for it. Instead we use bottled clam juice. I'll send you the original recipe with our modifications to make it easier. This recipe makes about 6 to 7 quarts. It does freeze very well; but you can always half the recipe if you don't want to make so much. The big chore is cutting up all the vegetables, that's why I make a whole bunch at one time and just freeze it. Unfortunately you can't chop the vegetables in the food processer because thy come out too fine and mushy.

In a large pot which I have sprayed with PAM (to make cleaning it easier) cook the bacon, When it is fairly brown, add the onions and cook until golden brown, mixing as needed. Then add the crushed tomatoes and the tomato juice, 2 bottles of clam juice, diced celery, peppers, and carrots. Cook until the carrots are soft. Then add the diced potatoes and oregano and cook until the potatoes are soft.. If the soup looks too thick, may add 1 to 2 more bottles of clam juice. We have always liked the soup rather thick, so I rarely have to add more clam juice. Lower the heat to a simmer and add the chopped clams without covering for 2 minutes. Let cool and then refrigerate or freeze.

I usually peel all the vegetables except for the potatoes before I start cooking and then dice the potatoes while I'm cooking and waiting for the carrots to get soft. The potatoes need to be kept covered with cold water as you are working with them so they don't turn brown. This always tastes better if made one to two days in advance and refrigerated.
